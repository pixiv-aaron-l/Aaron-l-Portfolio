This text collects the many nicknames, slang terms, and inside jokes used on Komica. 

Verbs (動詞)

右鍵 (Right-click)
Meaning: To save an image.
Origin: General website slang, most people save images by right-clicking

D・V・D!!
Meaning: To strip or take off one's clothes.
Origin: From the manga いけないお姉さん (Ikenai Onee-san). The phrase became a meme on Japanese imageboards and spread to Komica.

Ｃ・Ｑ・Ｃ!!
Meaning: To tackle someone or pin them down.
Origin: "Close Quarters Combat" (CQC) from the Metal Gear Solid series. In the games, enemies knocked unconscious with CQC can be completely disarmed.

原創風行 (Original Trend)
Meaning: Plagiarism.
Origin: Refers to a famous Komica incident where someone who promoted "originality" was exposed as copying others.

轉學 (Transfer schools)
Meaning: To die or be killed.
Origin: Comes from the euphemism "他‧轉‧學‧了" (He... transferred schools.) but it's used humorously instead of saying someone died.

帶回家 (Take home)
Meaning: To kidnap or abduct someone.
Origin: Based on the character Ryūgū Rena from Higurashi no Naku Koro ni, who often says she wants to "take cute things home."

SLB
Meaning: To eliminate, destroy, or kill someone completely.
Origin: Originally just shorthand for completely wiping someone out. Later, because of Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha, it also became associated with Nanoha's ultimate attack, Starlight Breaker.

水果 / 芭樂 (Fruit / Guava)
Meaning: False information, fake leaks, fabricated rumors.
Origin: Common ACG forum slang. "throwing a guava" also means throwing a grenade, since guava is a Taiwanese slang for a hand grenade.

叫床 (Jiàochuáng)
Meaning: To wake someone up.
Origin: Literally, it is simply an abbreviation of "calling someone out of bed." However, in Mainland China, the term is usually understood to mean "moaning during sex", this usage is meant to cause confusion.

補魔力 (Restore magical energy)
Meaning: To have sex.
Origin: From Fate/stay night. In the original story, magical energy can be transferred through sexual intercourse, leading "mana replenishment" to become an euphemism for sex in the fandom.

放大絕 (Use the ultimate move)
Meaning: To resort to the argument "Then why don't you do it yourself?" or "Go make it yourself!"
Origin: Usually appears during heated anime or manga debates. When someone is losing an argument, they'll often pull out this to criticism.

讓頭腦冷靜 (Cool your head down)
Meaning: To attack someone.
Origin: From the Japanese line "少し…頭冷やそうか…" (Let's cool your head down a little...), usually spoken immediately before beating someone up.

L5
Meaning: To go insane.
Origin: Refers to Level 5 of Hinamizawa Syndrome from Higurashi no Naku Koro ni, the stage where victims completely lose their sanity.

上車 (Get on the bus / Get in the car)
Meaning: An image contains nudity or explicit content.
Origin: Early Bahamut forum posts containing adult material would be sent to the site's "A Board" (Abuse Board) as punishment. This eventually evolved into the expression "getting on the bus," meaning the content is NSFW.

Out
Meaning: The image contains visible nudity.
Origin: Borrowed from Japanese fandom terminology, though Komica developed its own interpretation.

Safe
Meaning: Technically doesn't show nipples or genitals, but is otherwise extremely revealing.
Origin: A common joke is that people keep calling obviously explicit images "Safe" just because the critical parts aren't visible.
Common reply: "It was just a mosquito bite." (This jokes that the visible nipples are supposedly just mosquito bites)

糟糕 (Terrible / Awkward)
Meaning: Lewd, perverted, or sexually suggestive.
Origin: A translation of the Japanese fandom expression often used to refer to erotic or "dirty-minded" content.

GJ
Meaning: Good job.
Origin: Simply the abbreviation of "Good Job."

撿肥皂 (Pick up the soap)
Meaning: To expose one's butt, by implication is to be the victim of prison-style sexual assault.
Origin: Military and prison slang that also became common internet humor.

肛
Meaning: Anal sex.
Origin: Mainland Chinese internet users often use the phrase "爆菊" ("explode the chrysanthemum") for the same meaning.

雜碎 (Trash / Scum)
Meaning: Ironically refers to someone who has won at life, it usually refers to an enviably lucky protagonist or person.
Origin: A sarcastic Komica meme: "Everyone curses the bastard, but everyone secretly wishes they were him."

幻術 (Genjutsu / Illusion Technique)
Meaning: "Don't worry, the author will fix it next chapter. (Usually they won't)"
Origin: From Naruto, during Sasuke and Itachi's battle, several chapters turned out to be nothing more than illusions, leading readers to joke that any shocking development could simply be "the author's genjutsu."

河蟹 (River Crab)
Meaning: To censor, filter, or remove content.
Origin: This comes from the Chinese government's slogan of building a "Harmonious Society" (和諧社會). Since "harmony" (和諧, héxié) became associated with censorship, Chinese internet users replaced it with the similar-sounding "river crab" (河蟹, héxiè). Eventually, even "river crab" itself began being censored, so people jokingly replaced it again with 水產 (aquatic products/seafood).

水產 (Seafood / Aquatic Products)
Meaning: Another euphemism for censorship.
Origin: See 河蟹 above.

歡樂惡搞3 (Funny Spoofs 3)
Meaning: Animals and pets.
Origin: On Komica, board numbers often become shorthand for their content. "Funny Spoofs 3" gradually became synonymous with the animals/pets board.

歡樂惡搞4 (Funny Spoofs 4) / 蘿莉2 (Loli 2) / 超長現象、超蘿現象
Meaning: Supernatural or paranormal phenomena.
Origin: These are community nicknames that developed because of the board's posting habits and recurring memes. They became widely accepted among Komica users.

歡樂惡搞5 (Funny Spoofs 5)
Meaning: MythBusters.
Origin: Another community nickname for that board.

歡樂惡搞6 (Funny Spoofs 6)
Meaning: Jokes.
Origin: After Komica reorganized its boards, this number became almost universally recognized as referring to the jokes board.

詳細希望 (Detailed information, please.)
Meaning: "Source, please." Equivalent to: "Source?" , "Sauce?"

楊培安 (Yang Pei-an)
Meaning: Another way of asking for the source.
Origin: Yang Pei-an is the singer of the song 《我相信》 (I Believe). One lyric goes: 我相信自由自在 (I believe in freedom) and people jokingly altered it into 我詳細希望 (I hope for detailed information). Because 我相信 (I believe) and 我詳細希望 (I want detailed information) sound somewhat similar in Mandarin, it became a meme.

脫褲 (Take off your pants)
Meaning: To masturbate or have sex.
Origin: Obviously, unless you're already naked, taking off your pants is a necessary first step.

掏出 (Take it out / Pull it out)
Meaning: To pull out one's penis.
Origin: Usually left implied. Commonly appears as the setup for many adult jokes.

小兔子暗黑無限破 (Little Bunny Dark Infinite Break)
Meaning: An absurd or completely unexpected plot twist.
Origin: From 絕對靈域 (Absolute Territory), where this over-the-top attack name became symbolic of ridiculously exaggerated developments. Equivalent English expression: "The plot went completely off the rails".

UCCU
Meaning: "Look at you!" Usually used to tease someone after they've embarrassed themselves.
Origin: Chinesse "你看看你" is pronounced "You See See You" -> UCCU. Often accompanied by this emoticon: ☜(ﾟ∀ﾟ☜)

SAN值歸零 (SAN value reaches zero)
Meaning: To go completely insane. Nowadays it's commonly used whenever someone sees something horrifying or mentally scarring.
Origin: From the tabletop RPG Call of Cthulhu. SAN (Sanity) measures a character's mental stability. Reaching zero SAN means total insanity.

圓環之理 (The Law of Cycles)
Meaning: To disappear permanently.
Origin: Puella Magi Madoka Magica plot. People jokingly say something "returned to the Law of Cycles" when it vanishes forever.

學姐 (Senpai / Upperclassman)
Meaning: To be decapitated.
Origin: Also from Puella Magi Madoka Magica, Mami Tomoe (called Mami-senpai) famously has her head bitten off so "becoming senpai" has meant losing one's head.

真香 (Smells great / "Actually... this is good.")
Meaning: Someone completely reverses their position after confidently declaring they would never do something.
Origin: From the Chinese reality TV show 變形計 (X-Change). Participant Wang Jingze (王境澤) loudly declared: "Even if I starve to death, I would never eat your food." A short while later he happily ate it while saying: 真香 ("It really smells good.") His immediate hypocrisy turned into one of the biggest memes on the Chinese internet. Today, "真香" simply means someone contradicted themselves almost immediately.

潮 (Trendy / Cool / Stylish)
Meaning: Cool. Fashionable. Stylish. Popular.
Origin: The slang entered Taiwan from Hong Kong. It later became much more widespread because Tite Kubo (author of Bleach) frequently portrayed his characters with exaggeratedly fashionable clothing and poses. As a result, "very Bleach" and "very trendy (很潮)" became closely associated.

Nouns (名詞)

3886
Meaning: The founder of Komica.
Origin: 3886 is simply the handle (username) of Komica's creator. On Komica, mentioning "3886" almost always refers to the site's administrator. The name was taken from the post 3886 story.

股價 (Stock Price)
Meaning: A character's popularity or attractiveness.
Origin: From the manga かってに改蔵 (Katte ni Kaizō), where "stock price" was used as a metaphor for a person's value. Komica adapted it specifically to describe a character's "market value" among fans. "Her stock price is rising." "That episode crashed his stock."

丁丁 (Ding Ding / Teletubby Tinky Winky)
Meaning: An idiot, moron, or complete fool.
Origin: Refers to the famous Chinese internet meme: 丁丁是個人才 (Ding Ding is quite the 'talent'). The phrase is heavily sarcastic. Calling someone 丁丁 means they're astonishingly stupid.

熱狗 (Hot Dog)
Meaning: Penis.
Origin: A long-running internet euphemism.

兄貴 (Aniki / Big Brother)
Meaning: A muscular man. A hyper-masculine guy. Sometimes an over-the-top homosexual stereotype.
Origin: After the popularity of Billy Herrington and other Nico Nico Douga memes, 兄貴 became strongly associated with muscular gay men and bodybuilding culture. Unlike English "bro", it usually carries meme implications.

斷背山 (Brokeback Mountain)
Meaning: Male homosexuality (BL).
Origin: Named after the novel and Ang Lee's film Brokeback Mountain.

淫獸 (Lewd Beast)
Meaning: Ferret.
Origin: Refers to Yuno Scrya from Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha, fans jokingly call him a "perverted beast" because of the situations he often ends up in.

蘿蔔 (Radish)
Meaning: Giant humanoid robot (mecha).
Origin: A Chinese pun. (Robot -> Ro-bot -> 蘿蔔 (luóbo, "radish"), you'll often hear: "I like radish animes." meaning: "I like mecha animes."

黑歷史 (Black History)
Meaning: An embarrassing past. Something you'd rather forget ever happened.
Origin: From Turn A Gundam, where the "Black History" referred to the forgotten, catastrophic past of civilization. Nowadays it's used exactly like "cringe phase."

F91
Meaning: Large breasts.
Origin: From Comic Party. One heroine, Mizuki Takase, has measurements beginning with an F-cup and 91 cm bust, leading to "F91." It also intentionally references the Gundam title Mobile Suit Gundam F91, making it a layered nerd joke.

便當 (Lunch Box / Boxed Lunch)
Meaning: Death. A character destined to die, receiving a death flag.
Origin: In Japanese media, a dead soldier often leaves behind an uneaten lunch box (弁当). Over time "Getting a lunch box" became synonymous with "Getting killed".

オワタ (Owata)
Meaning: "It's over." "I'm screwed." "We're doomed."
Origin: Japanese internet slang. Derived from 終わった ("It's over"). The famous ASCII art character ＼(^o^)／ is called オワタ (Owata) because it looks like someone giving up.

兵變 (Military Betrayal)
Meaning: A soldier's girlfriend cheats on him while he's serving in the military.
Origin: Based on the joke: "You're fighting on the front lines. I'm rebelling in the rear." Because Taiwan has mandatory military service, this became a notorious stereotype.

叉燒包 (Barbecued Pork Bun)
Meaning: A broken image icon.
Origin: Internet Explorer 3 displayed a broken image as a red X, people thought it resembled a char siu bun. Modern browsers no longer use that icon, but the nickname survived.

好人 (Good Guy)
Meaning: Someone rejected after confessing their love.
Origin: From the classic rejection phrase "You're a really good person..." 

好人卡 (Good Guy Card)
Meaning: The figurative "card" you receive after being friend-zoned.
Origin: See above.

爬山 (Mountain Climbing)
Meaning: Disappearing from public view or committing suicide. 
Origin: Chinesse slang to avoid censor.

做碰友
Meaning: To engage in BL (Boys' Love).
Origin: A wordplay combining "friend" and references to gay-themed works, including Brokeback Mountain.

逼
Meaning: A censored word.
Origin: In parody anime and TV shows, copyrighted names are often partially censored with a "beep."

孔明 (Kongming)
Meaning: A hidden trap.
Origin: From the famous Mario meme 孔明の罠 (Kongming's Trap), named after Zhuge Liang (Kongming), legendary Chinese strategist.

Nice Boat
Meaning: A television broadcast replaced due to censorship or unexpected real-world events.
Origin: One of the most famous anime memes ever. The final episode of School Days was preempted in Japan after a real murder involving an axe occurred. Instead of the episode, viewers saw peaceful scenery eventualy including a boat. The words "Nice boat." became synonymous with: Unexpected censorship.

瑞穗 (Mizuho)
Meaning: A crossdressing boy.
Origin: The heroine Mizuho Miyanokōji from Otoboku: Maidens Are Falling For Me! For many years, "Mizuho" was the default nickname for feminine boys before characters like Watarase Jun became more famous.

SLB
Meaning: An overwhelmingly destructive attack.
Origin: Starlight Breaker, Nanoha Takamachi's signature ultimate attack in Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha. Calling something "SLB" implies overwhelming, excessive firepower. "He solved the argument with an SLB." means "He completely obliterated the opposition.".

踢牙老奶奶 (Kick-the-Teeth Grandma)
Meaning: A hilariously bad mistranslation.
Origin: The Chinese localization of Neverwinter Nights by Interplay's Chinese publisher became infamous for catastrophic translation errors. Among them was a translation so absurd that "Kick-the-Teeth Grandma" became shorthand for any spectacularly terrible localization. Unlike ordinary translation mistakes, this refers specifically to errors so bad they're legendary.

毛子 (Hairies / Hairy Ones)
Meaning: Russia or Russians.
Origin: A nickname originating in northeastern China, based on the stereotype that Russians are very hairy. It can be neutral, humorous, or derogatory.

鐵幕 (Iron Curtain)
Meaning: Russia or the Soviet Union.
Origin: From Winston Churchill's famous 1946 "Iron Curtain" speech describing the division between Eastern and Western Europe during the Cold War. Since the "Iron Curtain" referred largely to the Soviet sphere of influence, the term eventually became shorthand for Russia in internet slang.

太空強國 (Great Space Power)
Meaning: China.
Origin: A sarcastic nickname referring to China's growing space program.

天朝 (Celestial Empire)
Meaning: China.
Origin: Historically, Chinese emperors regarded China as the center of civilization ("the Middle Kingdom"). Modern internet users often use 天朝 sarcastically to mock nationalism or excessive patriotism. Comparable to calling a country "The Glorious Empire."

東煌 (Eastern Radiance / Donghuang)
Meaning: China.
Origin: Originally became popular after the launch of Shenzhou 5 as a sarcastic nickname. It later gained new life through the game Azur Lane, where "Donghuang" is the faction representing China.

AV強國 (AV Superpower)
Meaning: Japan.
Origin: A joking reference to Japan's enormous adult video (AV) industry.

倭國 (Land of Wa)
Meaning: Japan.
Origin: An old Chinese name for Japan. Historically it was often used as a derogatory term, so modern users should be careful when using it.

昇陽帝國 (Empire of the Rising Sun)
Meaning: Japan.
Origin: The name used for Japan in the Command & Conquer: Red Alert 3 series.

11區 (Area 11)
Meaning: Japan.
Origin: From Code Geass. After conquering Japan, the Holy Britannian Empire renames it "Area 11." Anime fans adopted "11th District" as a nickname for Japan.

重櫻 (Sakura Empire)
Meaning: Japan.
Origin: From Azur Lane, where the Sakura Empire is based on Imperial Japan.

日不落國 (The Empire on Which the Sun Never Sets)
Meaning: The United Kingdom.
Origin: Refers to the British Empire at its height, when its colonies stretched across the globe, meaning the sun was always shining somewhere in British territory.

神聖不列顛尼亞帝國 (Holy Britannian Empire)
Meaning: The United Kingdom.
Origin: Although fictional, the Britannian Empire in Code Geass is heavily inspired by the British Empire.

皇家 (Royal / Royal Navy)
Meaning: The United Kingdom.
Origin: In Azur Lane, the Royal faction represents Britain.

美帝 (American Empire / Yankee Empire)
Meaning: The United States.
Origin: Short for "American Imperialism." Usually used sarcastically or politically. Depending on context it can criticize: American military intervention, Economic dominance, Exporting American culture, or perceived hypocrisy regarding democracy.

白鷹 (Eagle Union / White Eagle)
Meaning: The United States.
Origin: From Azur Lane, where Eagle Union represents the U.S.

第三帝國 (Third Reich)
Meaning: Germany.
Origin: According to Nazi ideology: Holy Roman Empire = First Reich, German Empire = Second Reich, Nazi Germany = Third Reich. Nowadays it's mostly used historically or humorously.

鐵血 (Ironblood)
Meaning: Germany.
Origin: Another Azur Lane faction, representing Germany.

立陶碗 (Lithuania Bowl)
Meaning: Lithuania.
Origin: A visual and phonetic pun. (立陶宛 (Lithuania) -> 立陶碗 (Lithuania Bowl), only one character changes).

Kyrios/Arios Maneuver
Meaning: To start an argument, deliver one devastating attack, then immediately leave before anyone can respond.
Origin: From Gundam 00. The Gundams Kyrios and Arios specialize in high-speed hit-and-run tactics. The phrase became a metaphor for people who post inflammatory comments and disappear. Comparable to modern internet phrases like: "Drop the mic and leave."

武力介入 (Armed Intervention)
Meaning: A moderator deleting posts.
Origin: Also from Gundam 00. Celestial Being repeatedly declares: "We will carry out armed intervention against all acts of war." Komica users joke that moderators perform "armed intervention" whenever they delete threads.

妹斗 / 妹抖
Meaning: Maid.
Origin: These are phonetic Chinese spellings of the Japanese pronunciation メイド (meido).

冥土 (Land of the Dead)
Meaning: Also "maid."
Origin: A pun (メイド, meido sounds identical to 冥土 meido, meaning "the underworld" or "the land of the dead"). People intentionally confuse the two.

K9
Meaning: Dog.
Origin: In English, K9 is pronounced exactly like canine. The joke simply carried over into Chinese fandom.

紳士 (Gentleman)
Meaning: A cultured pervert.
Origin: From the famous anime quote: "I'm not a pervert. Even if I were, I'd be a gentlemanly pervert." Unlike simply calling someone a "pervert," calling them a gentleman usually implies they appreciate erotic material with refined taste.

扶他那裡 (Futanari)
Meaning: A hermaphrodite or futanari character possessing both male and female sexual characteristics.
Origin: A phonetic transcription of the Japanese word ふたなり (futanari). Usually shortened simply to 扶他.

高達八成 (About 80% Certain)
Meaning: A completely baseless guess presented with false confidence.
Origin: A famous Komica meme. Someone would repeatedly say something was "about 80% certain" despite having absolutely no evidence. Today it's equivalent to saying: "I'm making this up." or "Source: trust me."

廚房 (Kitchen)
Meaning: An immature middle-school kid.
Origin: Japanese 厨房 (kitchen) and 中坊 (middle-schooler) are both pronounced chūbō. Internet users intentionally wrote the wrong characters. Over time "kitchen" became slang for an obnoxious, immature teenager.

消房
Meaning: Elementary school child.
Origin: A joke derived from 廚房. If 廚房 means middle-school kid, then 消房 humorously extends it to elementary schoolers.

午餐時間 (Lunchtime)
Meaning: A mass death scene.
Origin: An upgraded version of 便當 (boxed lunch). If one character gets a lunch box, they die. If everyone gets lunch at once... It's lunchtime. Meaning lots of characters die in the same episode.

KY
Meaning: Someone incapable of reading the room.
Origin: Japanese abbreviation 空気読め (Kuuki Yome) "Read the atmosphere". Calling someone KY means they're socially oblivious. Interestingly, on Komica 2 the abbreviation could also jokingly refer to personal lubricant, creating a double meaning.

可魯 (Quill)
Meaning: "My eyes!" "So bright I'm going blind."
Origin: From the Japanese guide dog Quill (可魯). People jokingly invoke the famous guide dog whenever something is overwhelmingly bright. Equivalent to: "My retinas are burning."

鄉民 (Villagers / Townsfolk)
Meaning: The crowd, bystanders, onlookers, or the internet masses.
Origin: From Stephen Chow's film Hail the Judge (九品芝麻官). One character says: "I was just joining the villagers to watch the excitement. I just happened to be standing closer to the front." Taiwanese internet users adopted 鄉民 to mean ordinary forum users or the online crowd. Comparable to: "The peanut gallery." , "The internet." , "The crowd."

中出 (Nakadashi / Finish Inside)
Meaning: To ejaculate inside a partner.
Origin: Literally borrowed from the Japanese word 中出す (nakadasu). Unlike many slang terms, this is simply a direct borrowing.

中田氏 (Mr. Nakata)
Meaning: Also "to ejaculate inside."
Origin: A visual pun. Japanese なかだし (nakadashi) written as Chinese characters: 中田氏. These characters normally read "Mr. Nakata," but here they're used solely because they resemble the pronunciation of nakadashi. It's one of the oldest Japanese internet wordplays adopted into Chinese fandom.

sage (also written Sage)
Meaning: To intentionally keep a thread from being bumped.
Origin: Imageboards like 2channel allow users to type sage into the email field. Doing so posts a reply without moving the thread to the top. Because of this, people also say things like: "Sage this thread." Meaning: "Don't bump it."

公號9527 (Number 9527)
Meaning: "LZSB" (The Original Poster is an idiot).
Origin: If you flip 9527 upside down, some people think it vaguely resembles the Chinese characters: 樓主傻逼 (The thread starter is a dumbass.) This joke isn't obvious unless you're familiar with the stylized numeral meme.

蘭州燒餅 (Lanzhou Flatbread)
Meaning: "LZSB."
Origin: Each Chinese character's pinyin initials are: 蘭 (L) 州 (Z) 燒 (S) 餅 (B). This is one of the oldest acronym jokes on the Chinese internet.

YOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO
Meaning: A crossdressing boy, feminine-looking male, or "trap."
Origin: From Japanese internet culture. The exaggerated "YOOOOOO" shout became associated with unexpectedly discovering that an attractive "girl" is actually male. The term is largely considered dated today and reflects older internet slang.

自演乙 (Good job acting by yourself.)
Meaning: A sarcastic way of accusing someone of pretending to be multiple people. Equivalent to: "Nice sockpuppet." , "Forgot to switch accounts?"
Origin: From 2channel. Japanese 自作自演お疲れ様 (Good work staging that yourself.), the 乙 (otsu) is shorthand for お疲れ様 (good work).

今北產業 (Just got here—three-line summary.)
Meaning: "Can someone summarize what's happening?" Equivalent to: TL;DR? Catch me up.
Origin: From 2channel. Original Japanese 今きた三行で説明して (I just arrived. Explain it in about three lines.) over time 今北産業 became a fixed meme because it sounds similar when spoken aloud.

SAN值 (SAN Value)
Meaning: A person's sanity.
Origin: From the tabletop RPG Call of Cthulhu. SAN represents mental stability.

Unlike SAN值歸零, which means going insane, SAN值 itself simply refers to one's remaining sanity.

好人卡 (Good Guy Card)
Meaning: The figurative "certificate" of being friend-zoned.
Origin: When someone is rejected with "You're a really good person..." they're jokingly said to have received a Good Guy Card. Unlike simply saying 好人, 好人卡 specifically emphasizes romantic rejection.

邪神像 (Evil God Statue)
Meaning: An unbelievably ugly figure or PVC model.
Origin: Originally, the term literally meant a statue of an evil deity. However, anime fans began using it for merchandise whose sculpt or paint job was so catastrophically bad that it resembled some unknowable eldritch horror. Especially common for: Poorly painted faces, Deformed proportions, Cheap bootleg figures. The joke is that the figure has become something worthy of worship—not because it's beautiful, but because it's so horrifying.

德國骨科 (German Orthopedics)
Meaning: Incest between siblings.
Origin: One of China's most famous internet stories. According to the meme, a member of a scanlation group had sex with his biological younger sister. Their father supposedly broke his leg. He went to Germany for orthopedic treatment. Rumors later claimed the siblings even legally married in Germany. Whether any of this actually happened is doubtful, but the phrase survived as shorthand for sibling romance. Today you'll often see: "This belongs in German Orthopedics." meaning "This is sibling incest".

奇蹟似的七人 (The Miraculous Seven)
Meaning: A harem ending where every heroine has the protagonist's children.
Origin: From Tosh's manga ハーレムタイム (Harem Time). The ending famously features all seven heroines giving birth to the protagonist's children at roughly the same time. Because of its sheer absurdity, it became legendary among harem manga fans.

万策尽きた！ (All Plans Exhausted!) Also translated as: "Out of options!" "We've tried everything!" "We're screwed!"
Meaning: A production disaster.
Origin: From the anime SHIROBAKO. Whenever production manager Honda Toyotaka realizes the animation schedule is collapsing, he exclaims: 万策尽きた！ Fans now use the phrase whenever: An anime is delayed, Animation quality collapses, Production obviously falls apart, A game misses release, Any major project enters development hell

祭壇 (Altar)
Meaning: A room corner completely covered in merchandise dedicated to one character, voice actor, idol, or franchise.
Origin: Fans arrange figures, posters, acrylic stands, plushes, CDs, and other collectibles almost like a religious shrine. People jokingly call these displays "altars". This usage is still extremely common throughout anime fandom.

Particles / Internet Expressions (語助詞)
The article now moves on to small expressions and internet shorthand rather than nouns or verbs.

(ry
Meaning: "...and so on." "...the rest omitted." / Equivalent to: "etc." "you know the rest."
Origin: From the Japanese word 略 (りゃく, ryaku) meaning "omitted." People simply write the first two letters: (ry

w
Meaning: "Lol."
Origin: Japanese 笑う (warau) meaning "to laugh". The initial w became shorthand for laughter. The more w's, the harder someone is laughing. It serves essentially the same role as lol or lmao in English.

LOL
Meaning: "Laughing Out Loud."
Origin: The standard English internet abbreviation. Unlike w, which comes from Japanese internet culture, LOL is borrowed directly from English-speaking internet communities.

アル (Aru)
Meaning: A stereotypical sentence ending used for Chinese characters in Japanese fiction.
Origin: Many older Japanese works have Chinese characters end sentences with アル. Example: That's just an illusion, aru. The stereotype probably originated from the Mandarin -er (兒) suffix or early Japanese impressions of Chinese speech. Modern audiences generally recognize it as an anime cliché rather than realistic Chinese.

乙 (Otsu)
Meaning: "Good work." , "Nicely done." , "Thanks for your effort."
Origin: From 2channel. It abbreviates: お疲れ様 (otsukaresama) meaning "Thank you for your hard work." , "Good job."
